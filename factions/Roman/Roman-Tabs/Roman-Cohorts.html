Cohorts

General Information

The cohorts are typically groups of forty legionnaires who serve the Legion. Each cohort is headed by 2 centurions who control the 4 barracks with ten bunks in each used by their cohort. The Twelfth Legion Fulminata(Camp Jupiter in California) has five cohorts, however Ninth Legion Ignerata only has 4, leaving out the first cohort out of respect for the original Camp Jupiter. The cohorts are ranked from best (2) to worst (5) imitating the ancient Roman cohort structure. Campers may bring letters of recommendations to camp to get into a good cohort - the better the letter, the better the chance of them making it into the better cohorts.

Description

The cohorts have shady porches where campers lounge in hammocks, play cards, and drink sodas. Each barrack has a collection of banners displaying their cohort number in Roman Numerals and their cohort's animal.

Cohort Information

Second Cohort

Highest of the cohorts and most renowned for its prestige, the Second Cohort is comprised of senior legionnaires or those with exceptional recommendations either from Lupa or from retired legionnaires that speak for them.

Third Cohort

Regarded as the baseline cohort, for legionnaires, as the 'mediocre' fighter category in some cases, although its legionnaires tend to be the more versatile types. Most times, people are transferred to this cohort and not placed there by recommendation letter.

Fourth Cohort

The infamous troublemaking cohort of camp, the Fourth is composed of people who are working their way up from the Fifth, or those deemed particularly mischievous.

Fifth Cohort

Every legionnaire without a sufficient enough recommendation to warrant a transfer to a higher cohort is placed in the Fifth. Probatio legionnaires stay here as well until assigned to a cohort.
