Odin

King of the Gods; deity of war, wisdom, and magic. Known as the All-Father, and the patron of rulers, wanderers, and outcasts. In appearance, he is capable of taking on any appearance, but most of his appearances involve gray hair and/or a staff-like implement. Odin is famous for advising heroes or watching others through his ravens or through the eyes of others.

Frigg

Queen of the Gods; deity of prophecy, marriage, and motherhood. As the wife of Odin, she is also known as the All-Mother, and the patron of all women as well. Appearance wise, Frigg's form shifts between the many different ages of a woman, with her only defining trait being the gift of prophecy (although she'll never tell anyone what she's seen).

Thor

God of thunder and strength. Child of Odin and husband of Sif; he is famous for his hammer Mjolnir, and appears often as a god with red hair, a red beard, and a generally large and raggedy appearance. He is also typically followed by his two goats and can be counted on to completely miss the point or have anger issues at any given moment.

Loki

Jotun, ex-deity, and patron of tricksters, fire, and snakes. Husband of Sigyn, and parent to Hel, Jormungandr, and Fenrir. Famous for his various 'pranks' and crafty plots despite their inclination to cruelty, as well as his tendency to be able to persuade people to do almost anything. He's not known for much else than chaos and shapeshifting, and can appear as virtually anyone or anything.

Sif

Deity of the earth and harvest, Sif is most characteristically known for her long blonde hair, said to be pure gold after a prank with Loki resulted in the loss of her hair - he conned a few dwarves into making her a golden wig. Also known for being the wife of Thor - and for being able to make almost anything from her hair.

Ran

One of multiple oceanic deities, Ran collects the souls of the drowned in her magical net. She appears as an old woman wearing a net as a skirt, and is known for her hoarding problem. Wife of Aegir, although she's not happy about it. Although most of her net's contents are souls and junk, she occasionally picks up other valuable things.

Freyr

Twin brother to Freyja and god of peace, summer, and wealth, Freyr is the son of Njord and the husband of Gerd, a jotun. Known for his sword, Sumarbrandar, which he traded away for his wife's bride-price - and the fact that it will kill him in Ragnarok, the ending of the world. Appears normally as a blonde man with blue eyes, and a calming aura.

Freyja

Twin sister to Freyr and deity of love, war, and sorcery as well as the patron saint of cats, Freyja is one of the most powerful users of magic in this pantheon. She presides over Folkvangr, one of the realms of the dead, and is known for being one of the most beautiful of the Norse gods, as well as her ability to cry golden tears.

Heimdallr

Watchman of the gods, and deity of vigilance and light, Heimdallr keeps watch for the onset of Ragnarok at the end of the Bifrost in Asgard. He seems to be obsessed with pictures although he is able to search the nine worlds from his post at the edge of the Bifrost and perform his duties when necessary.

Tyr

God of war, courage, and justice, Tyr is famous for sacrificing his right hand to keep Fenris Wolf bound. He is one of the more benevolent deities who is described as noble and brave despite not appearing much in canon. Tyr is described as tall, with black armor and dark skin, as well as a golden cap on his right wrist.

Idunn

Keeper of the apples of immortality, Idunn is the deity of spring and eternal youth. These apples have become a source of conflict between the gods, as they retain the youthfulness of the gods, and can be eaten by almost anyone should they get their hands on it - even though these apples do not prevent death, they stay it.
